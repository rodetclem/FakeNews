Eiffel Tower to be Disassembled : Paris Takes Drastic Mesures to Combat Overtourism

By Richard Fontaineau

Updated on August 4th 2023

This morning, an unprecedented decision was made. The City of Paris has announced plans to deconstruct its iconic monument. This comes after months of discussion regarding the overwhelming challenges posed by mass tourism in the city.

Mass tourism caused havocs in the capital. The ever growing concerns over its consequences such as congestion, wear and tear of the structure and environmental impact, ultimately pushed forward this baffling resolution.

Anne Hidalgo, the city's mayor, explained the decision in a press conference: "The Eiffel Tower has become a victim of its own success. Overtourism is straining our city, and this move is necessary to preserve the well-being of Parisians and our cultural heritage."

The delicate disassembly process, is to be led by experts in engineering and preservation, and aims to potentially repurpose each piece of the monument. While some stakeholders worry about the economic consequences, others see this as an opportunity for Paris to redefine its tourism model and prioritize sustainability.
